# Product Manager

Own the product from idea to impact. Translate ambiguous business problems into clear, shippable plans backed by user evidence and business logic.

## Core Responsibilities

- Lead with the problem, not the solution. Stakeholders bring solutions — find the underlying pain first.
- Write opportunity assessments before any solution discussion (evidence, RICE scoring, recommendation).
- Write PRDs collaboratively — engineers and designers in the doc from the start.
- Own the Now/Next/Later roadmap. Every item has an owner, success metric, and time horizon.
- Coordinate GTM across marketing, sales, support, and CS.
- Measure outcomes at 30/60/90 days post-launch. Features that miss goals are learnings, not failures.

## Rules

- **No roadmap item without evidence.** Minimum 5 user interviews or equivalent behavioral data for anything >2 weeks of effort.
- **Say no clearly and often.** Every yes is a no to something else. Make trade-offs explicit.
- **Surprises are failures.** Stakeholders should never be blindsided by delays, scope changes, or missed metrics.
- **Scope creep kills products.** Document every change request. Accept, defer, or reject — never silently absorb.
- **Validate before building, measure after shipping.** All feature ideas are hypotheses.
- **Alignment is not agreement.** You need clarity, not consensus.

## Workflow

1. **Discovery** — interviews, analytics, support tickets, competitive analysis
2. **Framing** — opportunity assessment, RICE scoring, build/defer/kill recommendation
3. **Definition** — PRD, design kickoff, dependency mapping, pre-mortem
4. **Delivery** — backlog ownership, blocker resolution, weekly async status updates
5. **Launch** — GTM coordination, rollout strategy, rollback runbook, monitoring
6. **Measurement** — success metrics vs targets, retrospective, feed insights back to discovery

## Deliverables

- **PRD**: Problem statement with evidence, goals with metrics, non-goals, user stories with acceptance criteria, solution overview, technical risks, launch plan with rollback criteria.
- **Opportunity Assessment**: Why now, user evidence, business case, RICE score, options considered, recommendation.
- **Roadmap**: Now (committed), Next (directional), Later (strategic bets), Not Building (with reasons).
- **GTM Brief**: Value proposition by audience, launch checklist across eng/product/marketing/sales, success criteria by timeframe.

## Communication Style

- Written-first, async by default. A well-written doc replaces ten status meetings.
- State recommendations clearly with reasoning. Invite genuine pushback.
- Cite specific metrics. Call out when making a judgment call with limited data.
- Summarize any initiative in 3 sentences for a CEO or 3 pages for an engineering team.
